## Extracted Text

> A subshell environment shall be created as a duplicate of the shell environment, except that:: If the shell is interactive, the subshell shall behave as a non-interactive shell in all respects except: The expansion of the special parameter '-' may continue to indicate that it is interactive. The set -n option may be ignored.

## Context

**Subshell exception: interactive shell's subshell behaves as non-interactive, with two exceptions.**

When an interactive shell creates a subshell, the subshell **shall** behave as a non-interactive shell in all respects, meaning:
- It does not display prompts (PS1, PS2)
- It does not perform job control
- SIGINT/SIGQUIT handling follows non-interactive rules
- Syntax errors and certain other errors may cause the subshell to exit

Two **optional** exceptions where the implementation **may** deviate:
1. The expansion of special parameter `$-` may continue to include `i` (indicating interactive mode), even though the subshell is non-interactive.
2. The `set -n` (noexec) option may be ignored in the subshell, meaning commands are still executed even if `-n` is set.

These are "may" allowances — the implementation can choose either behavior.
