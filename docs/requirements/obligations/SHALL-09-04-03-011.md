## Extracted Text

> An ERE special character has special properties in certain contexts. Outside those contexts, or when preceded by an unescaped <backslash>, such a character shall be an ERE that matches the special character itself. The extended regular expression special characters and the contexts in which they shall have their special meaning are as follows:: The <circumflex> shall be special when used as an anchor (see 9.4.9 ERE Expression Anchoring ). The <circumflex> shall signify a non-matching list expression when it occurs first in a list, immediately following a <left-square-bracket> (see 9.3.5 RE Bracket Expression).

## Context

The `^` character has two distinct special contexts: (1) As an anchor (Section 9.4.9): outside a bracket expression, `^` anchors the expression or subexpression it begins to the start of the string. (2) As a negation operator (Section 9.3.5): when `^` is the first character after `[` in a bracket expression (i.e., `[^...]`), it creates a non-matching list that matches any character NOT in the specified set. In all other positions, `^` matches itself literally. The ERE engine must distinguish these two contexts correctly.
