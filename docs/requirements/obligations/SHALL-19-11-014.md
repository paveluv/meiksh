## Extracted Text

> A background job that was created on execution of an asynchronous AND-OR list can be brought into the foreground by means of the fg utility (if supported); in this case the entire job shall become a single foreground job. If a process that the shell subsequently waits for is part of this foreground job and is stopped by a signal, the entire job shall become a suspended job and the behavior shall be as if the process had been stopped while the job was running in the background.

## Context

**`fg` utility — bringing background jobs to foreground:** A background job created by an asynchronous AND-OR list can be brought to the foreground using the `fg` builtin (see `utilities/fg.html`). When this happens:
1. The entire job becomes a single foreground job.
2. If a process the shell waits for (now foreground) is stopped by a signal, the entire job becomes a suspended job, behaving as if the process had been stopped while the job was a background job.

The implementation must support `fg` to change a background job's status, call `tcsetpgrp()` to give it terminal access, send SIGCONT if stopped, and then wait for it as a foreground job. If any process in the newly-foregrounded job stops, the job becomes suspended (see SHALL-19-11-022 ff.).
