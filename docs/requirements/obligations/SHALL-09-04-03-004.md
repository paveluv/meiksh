## Extracted Text

> An ERE special character has special properties in certain contexts. Outside those contexts, or when preceded by an unescaped <backslash>, such a character shall be an ERE that matches the special character itself. ## Context

The right-parenthesis `)` is an ERE special character. Its special meaning (closing a grouping subexpression) applies only when it is matched with a preceding `(` and both are outside any bracket expression. When `)` appears without a matching `(`, or inside a bracket expression, it loses its special meaning. When preceded by an unescaped backslash (`\)`), it matches the literal `)` character. The ERE parser must track parenthesis nesting to determine whether `)` has special or literal meaning.
