> An ERE special character has special properties in certain contexts. ## Context

ERE special characters have special meaning only in their designated contexts (listed in subsections for each character: `.[\(`, `)`, `*+?{`, `|`, `^`, `$`). When an ERE special character appears outside its special context, or is preceded by an unescaped backslash, the ERE engine must treat it as matching the literal character itself. For example, `\*` matches a literal asterisk, and `*` inside a bracket expression matches a literal asterisk. The implementation must correctly identify each special character's contexts and fall back to literal matching in all other situations.
