## Extracted Text

> A sequence of characters starting with a <dollar-sign> immediately followed by a single-quote ($') shall preserve the literal value of all characters up to an unescaped terminating single-quote ('), with the exception of certain <backslash>-escape sequences, as follows:

## Context

**Behavior:** The `$'...'` quoting form preserves the literal value of all characters between the `$'` and the terminating unescaped `'`, except that certain backslash-escape sequences are recognized and replaced with the characters/bytes they represent. This is a quoting mechanism (like single-quotes or double-quotes) that additionally supports C-style escape sequences.

**Applies to:** All shell modes. The tokenizer must recognize `$'` as beginning a dollar-single-quote when the `$` is unquoted and immediately followed by `'`. This is distinct from a bare `$` followed by a separate single-quoted string.

**Key properties:**
- No parameter expansion, command substitution, or arithmetic expansion occurs inside `$'...'`.
- The `$` and enclosing single-quotes are consumed (not part of the result).
- The escape sequences are enumerated in sibling obligations SHALL-19-02-04-002 through SHALL-19-02-04-016.
- Escape processing happens before word expansion (see SHALL-19-02-04-018).

**Interaction with double-quotes:** Inside double-quotes, `$'` does NOT trigger dollar-single-quote processing (the `$` retains only its expansion-introducing meaning per SHALL-19-02-03-002).

**Spec reference:** Section 2.2.4 Dollar-Single-Quotes, lines 164-165 in `utilities/V3_chap02.html`.
