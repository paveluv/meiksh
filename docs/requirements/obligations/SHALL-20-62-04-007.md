## Extracted Text

> By default, the jobs utility shall display the status of all background jobs, both running and suspended, and all jobs whose status has changed and have not been reported by the shell.

## Context

**Behavior:** When `jobs` is invoked without `-l` or `-p` and without specific `job_id` operands, it must display:
1. All running background jobs.
2. All suspended (stopped) background jobs.
3. All jobs whose status has changed since the shell last reported them (e.g., jobs that have terminated but not yet been reported to the user via the shell's async notification).

**Default output format:** `"[%d] %c %s %s\n", <job-number>, <current>, <state>, <command>` (see SHALL-20-62-10-002).

**Spec reference:** POSIX.1-2024, `jobs` OPTIONS, line 60 of `utilities/jobs.html`.
