## Extracted Text

> The c_lflag field of the argument structure is used to control various functions. It is composed of the bitwise-inclusive OR of the masks shown, which shall be bitwise-distinct. The mask name symbols in this table are defined in <termios.h>.

## Context

**Behavior:** The `c_lflag` field controls various terminal functions and is composed of the bitwise-inclusive OR of masks that shall be bitwise-distinct. Mask names (ECHO, ECHOE, ECHOK, ECHONL, ICANON, IEXTEN, ISIG, NOFLSH, TOSTOP) are defined in `<termios.h>`.

**Relevance to meiksh:** The `stty` builtin must correctly read/set all `c_lflag` bits. Local mode flags are critical for the shell's interactive behavior — ICANON and ECHO directly affect line editing, and ISIG controls signal generation from special characters.

**Spec reference:** POSIX.1-2024, §11.2.5 Local Modes (tag_11_02_05), `basedefs/V1_chap11.html` lines 1087–1089.
