## Extracted Text

> If a filename begins with a <period> ('.'), the <period> shall be explicitly matched by using a <period> as the first character of the pattern or immediately following a <slash> character. The leading <period> shall not be matched by: The <asterisk> or <question-mark> special characters A bracket expression containing a non-matching list, such as "[!a]", a range expression, such as "[%-0]", or a character class expression, such as "[[:punct:]]" It is unspecified whether an explicit <period> in a bracket expression matching list, such as "[.abc]", can match a leading <period> in a filename.

## Context

**Duplicate of SHALL-19-14-03-002: leading dot in filenames must be explicitly matched.**

Same obligation as SHALL-19-14-03-002. A leading `.` in a filename **shall** only be matched by an explicit `.` in the pattern (at position 0 or immediately after `/`). `*`, `?`, and non-matching bracket expressions cannot match it. See SHALL-19-14-03-002 for full details.
