## Extracted Text

> The following rules shall be used to construct EREs matching multiple characters from EREs matching a single character:: When an ERE matching a single character or an ERE enclosed in parentheses is followed by the special character <plus-sign> ('+'), together with that <plus-sign> it shall match what one or more consecutive occurrences of the ERE would match. For example, the ERE "b+(bc)" matches the fourth to seventh characters in the string "acabbbcde". And, "[ab]+" and "[ab][ab]*" are equivalent.

## Context

The `+` operator must match one or more consecutive occurrences of the preceding single-character ERE or parenthesized ERE. It applies to the immediately preceding atom: a single character, a bracket expression, a period, an escaped character, or a parenthesized subexpression. The match is greedy (leftmost longest) by default. Examples: `b+` matches one or more `b` characters; `[ab]+` is equivalent to `[ab][ab]*`; `(bc)+` matches one or more occurrences of "bc". The `+` operator must not match zero occurrences (that is `?` or `*`).
