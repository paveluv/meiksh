## Extracted Text

> In the descriptions below, the exit status of some compound commands is stated in terms of the exit status of a compound-list. The exit status of a compound-list shall be the value that the special parameter '?' (see 2.5.2 Special Parameters) would have immediately after execution of the compound-list.

## Context

**Behavior:** When the spec describes a compound command's exit status in terms of a "compound-list", the exit status of that compound-list is the value that the special parameter `$?` would have immediately after executing the compound-list. In practice, this means the exit status of the **last command** executed within the compound-list.

**This matters for:** `if`, `while`, `until` (condition compound-lists), `for` (body compound-list), `case` (matched clause compound-list), brace groups, and subshell groups.

**Implementation:** After executing a compound-list (a sequence of AND-OR lists separated by `;` or `&`), the shell must capture the exit status as the value of `$?` at that point. This is what determines the exit status of the enclosing compound command.

**Spec reference:** Section 2.9.4 Compound Commands in `utilities/V3_chap02.html`, lines 2215-2217. Cross-ref: 2.5.2 Special Parameters for `$?`.
