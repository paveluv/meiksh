## Extracted Text

> Independent processes shall be capable of executing independently without either process terminating.

## Context

This is a duplicate extraction of the requirement that independent processes shall be capable of executing independently without either process terminating. After `fork()`, parent and child run concurrently and neither is required to terminate for the other to continue. For `meiksh`, this ensures that the shell can run commands in the background, manage pipelines with multiple concurrent processes, and not be blocked waiting for any single child to complete (except when semantically required, such as foreground job wait). See POSIX Utilities Chapter 1, Section 1.1.1.2 (utilities/V3_chap01.html line 74).
