## Extracted Text

> If job control is enabled (see set, -m), the AND-OR list shall become a job-control background job and a job number shall be assigned to it. If job control is disabled, the AND-OR list may become a non-job-control background job, in which case a job number shall be assigned to it; if no job number is assigned it shall become a background command but not a background job.

## Context

**Behavior:** Job control interaction with async AND-OR lists:
- **Job control enabled (`set -m`):** The AND-OR list MUST become a job-control background job, and a job number MUST be assigned.
- **Job control disabled:** The AND-OR list MAY become a non-job-control background job. If it does, a job number MUST be assigned. If no job number is assigned, it becomes a background command but NOT a background job (meaning `jobs` won't list it, but it still runs asynchronously).

**Applies to:** All `cmd &` constructs, with behavior depending on whether job control is enabled.

**Implementation detail:** When job control is enabled, create a new process group for the background job and assign a job number (e.g., [1], [2], etc.). When disabled, meiksh may choose whether to track it as a job. The `$!` variable must still be set regardless.

**Spec reference:** Section 2.9.3.1, line 2142 in `utilities/V3_chap02.html`. Cross-ref: `set -m`, 2.26 Declaration Utility.
