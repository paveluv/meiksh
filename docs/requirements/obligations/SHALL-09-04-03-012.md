## Extracted Text

> An ERE special character has special properties in certain contexts. Outside those contexts, or when preceded by an unescaped <backslash>, such a character shall be an ERE that matches the special character itself. The extended regular expression special characters and the contexts in which they shall have their special meaning are as follows:: $

## Context

The dollar-sign `$` is an ERE special character. Its special meaning is as an anchor that matches the end of a string (see Section 9.4.9). Outside of its anchoring context, or when preceded by an unescaped backslash, it matches the literal `$` character. Inside a bracket expression, `$` has no special meaning and matches itself. See SHALL-09-04-03-013 for the specific anchoring rule.
