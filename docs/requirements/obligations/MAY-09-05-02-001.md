## Extracted Text

> Note that although the BRE grammar appears always to permit L_ANCHOR or R_ANCHOR inside "\(" and "\)", the lexical conventions (see 9.5.1 BRE/ERE Grammar Lexical Conventions) imply that '^' and '$' may be ordinary characters there. This reflects the semantic limits on the application, as noted in 9.3.8 BRE Expression Anchoring. Since it is an implementation option whether to interpret '^' and '$' as anchors in these locations, conforming applications cannot use unescaped '^' and '$' in positions inside "\(" and "\)" that might be interpreted as anchors.

## Context

This is a note clarifying BRE anchor behavior inside subexpressions `\(...\)`. The BRE grammar syntactically allows `^` and `$` inside `\(...\)`, but the lexical conventions mean they MAY be treated as ordinary characters there. The implementation may choose to: (1) treat `^`/`$` as anchors inside BRE subexpressions, or (2) treat them as literal characters. Since this is implementation-optional, conforming applications cannot rely on either behavior. For `meiksh`, this affects how the BRE engine handles patterns like `\(^foo\)` or `\(foo$\)`. The chosen behavior should be documented.
