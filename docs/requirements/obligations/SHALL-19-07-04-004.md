## Extracted Text

> If any part of word is quoted, not counting double-quotes outside a command substitution if the here-document is inside one, the delimiter shall be formed by performing quote removal on word, and the here-document lines shall not be expanded. Otherwise:: The removal of <backslash><newline> for line continuation (see 2.2.1 Escape Character (Backslash)) shall be performed during the search for the trailing delimiter. (As a consequence, the trailing delimiter is not recognized immediately after a <newline> that was removed by line continuation.) It is unspecified whether the line containing the trailing delimiter is itself subject to this line continuation.

## Context

When the here-document delimiter is unquoted, backslash-newline line continuation must be processed during the search for the terminating delimiter. See 2.7.4 Here-Document in utilities/V3_chap02.html and 2.2.1 Escape Character (tag_19_02_01).

**Behavior required:**
- During the scan for the trailing delimiter line, `\<newline>` sequences (line continuations) SHALL be removed, joining the following line to the current one.
- As a consequence, the trailing delimiter is NOT recognized immediately after a newline that was removed by line continuation. For example, if a line ends with `\` and the next line is `EOF`, they are joined and `EOF` is not recognized as the delimiter on its own line.
- It is UNSPECIFIED whether the line containing the trailing delimiter itself is subject to line continuation processing.

**Implementation recommendation:** Process line continuation during the here-document body scan. For the delimiter line itself, either apply or don't apply line continuation — but document the choice. Most shells do not apply it to the delimiter line.
