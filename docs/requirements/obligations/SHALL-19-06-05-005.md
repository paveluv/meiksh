## Extracted Text

> Fields which contain no results from expansions shall not be affected by field splitting, and shall remain unaltered, simply moving from the list of input fields to be next in the list of output fields.

## Context

**Behavior:** If an input field contains NO expansion results (i.e., it is purely literal text with no bytes originating from parameter expansion, command substitution, or arithmetic expansion), it must pass through field splitting completely unchanged. It becomes the next output field as-is, with no splitting or modification.

**Inputs/Outputs:**
- In `echo hello world`, the words `hello` and `world` are literal (no expansions) and are not subject to field splitting at all — they pass through unchanged.
- In `echo hello${IFS}world`, the `hello` and `world` parts are literal but the field contains expansion results (from `${IFS}`), so the entire field IS subject to the splitting algorithm.

**Spec reference:** See 2.6.5 Field Splitting in utilities/V3_chap02.html, lines 1218–1219.
