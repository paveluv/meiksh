## Context

Outside of bracket expressions (`[...]`), the ERE engine must recognize exactly three constructs that each match a single character: (1) an ERE ordinary character, (2) an ERE special character, `]`, or `}` preceded by an unescaped backslash, and (3) a period (`.`). These are the only single-character matching atoms permitted outside bracket expressions. This rule defines the foundation for how the ERE parser classifies tokens when not inside `[...]`.
