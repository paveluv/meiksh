## Extracted Text

> An ERE special character has special properties in certain contexts. Outside those contexts, or when preceded by an unescaped <backslash>, such a character shall be an ERE that matches the special character itself. The extended regular expression special characters and the contexts in which they shall have their special meaning are as follows:: The <dollar-sign> shall be special when used as an anchor.

## Context

The `$` character is special only when used as an anchor (Section 9.4.9). As an anchor, `$` matches the end of a string (or the end of the line in line-oriented matching). In all other positions—inside bracket expressions or when preceded by an unescaped backslash—`$` matches the literal dollar-sign character. The ERE engine must identify the anchoring context for `$` (typically at the end of an ERE or subexpression) and treat it literally elsewhere.
