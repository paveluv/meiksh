## Extracted Text

> When not inside a bracket expression, the following shall match a single character:: an ERE special character, ']', or '}' preceded by an unescaped <backslash>

## Context

When not inside a bracket expression, a backslash-escaped ERE special character, `]`, or `}` must match the literal character that follows the backslash. The ERE special characters are `.`, `[`, `\`, `(`, `)`, `*`, `+`, `?`, `{`, `|`, `^`, `$`. For example, `\*` matches a literal asterisk, `\]` matches a literal right bracket, `\}` matches a literal right brace. The backslash itself must be unescaped (i.e., not preceded by another backslash that escapes it). Each such `\X` sequence constitutes a single-character-matching atom.
