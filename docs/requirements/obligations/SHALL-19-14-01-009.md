## Extracted Text

> When unquoted, unescaped, and not inside a bracket expression, the following three characters shall have special meaning in the specification of patterns:: A <question-mark> is a pattern that shall match any character.

## Context

**`?` matches any single character.**

An unquoted, unescaped `?` not inside a bracket expression is a pattern that **shall** match any single character. This includes any character in the supported character set except:
- NUL (which cannot appear in strings)
- In filename expansion context: `?` does not match `/` (see SHALL-19-14-03-001) and does not match a leading `.` (see SHALL-19-14-03-002)

Examples: `a?c` matches `abc`, `axc`, `a1c`, etc. but not `ac` or `abbc`.

In multi-byte locales, `?` matches one character (which may be multiple bytes).
