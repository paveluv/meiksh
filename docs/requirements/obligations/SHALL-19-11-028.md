## Context

**Background job stopped by signal → suspended job:** When a process associated with a background job is stopped by SIGSTOP, SIGTSTP, SIGTTIN, or SIGTTOU, the shell shall:
1. Convert the (non-suspended) background job into a suspended job in the job table.
2. An interactive shell shall write a status message to stderr, formatted as the `jobs` utility would for a suspended job (see `utilities/jobs.html`).

The timing of the message depends on the `set -b` option state (see SHALL-19-11-029 through SHALL-19-11-032). The shell detects stopped background processes via `waitpid()` with `WUNTRACED` flag, which reports stopped children in addition to terminated ones.
