## Extracted Text

> An ERE special character has special properties in certain contexts. Outside those contexts, or when preceded by an unescaped <backslash>, such a character shall be an ERE that matches the special character itself. The extended regular expression special characters and the contexts in which they shall have their special meaning are as follows:: .[\(

## Context

The characters `.`, `[`, `\`, and `(` are ERE special characters. Their special meaning applies everywhere except inside a bracket expression. The period matches any character (except NUL), the left-square-bracket begins a bracket expression, the backslash escapes the following character, and the left-parenthesis begins a grouping subexpression. When any of these four characters appears inside a bracket expression, it loses its special meaning and matches itself literally. When preceded by an unescaped backslash outside a bracket expression, each matches itself literally. See also SHALL-09-04-03-003 for the full rules including undefined-result cases.
