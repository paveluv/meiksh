## Extracted Text

> Opening a modem or similar piece of equipment connected by a serial line. In this case, the terminal parameters (see 11.2 Parameters that Can be Set) may be initialized to default settings by the implementation in between the last close of the device by any process and the next open of the device, or they may persist from one use to the next. The terminal parameters can be set to values that ensure the terminal behaves in a conforming manner by means of the O_TTY_INIT open flag when opening a terminal device that is not already open in any process, or by executing the stty utility with the operand sane.

## Context

**Behavior (optional):** Duplicate of MAY-11-01-01-001. Terminal parameters for serial devices may be reset to defaults or may persist between close/open cycles. `stty sane` or O_TTY_INIT can establish conforming behavior.

**Conditions:** Implementation-choice behavior for serial terminal parameter persistence.

**Shell relevance:** The `stty` builtin must implement the `sane` operand. The shell should function correctly regardless of whether terminal parameters are reset or persisted.
