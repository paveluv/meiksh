## Extracted Text

> A concatenation of EREs shall match the concatenation of the character sequences matched by each component of the ERE. A concatenation of EREs enclosed in parentheses shall match whatever the concatenation without the parentheses matches. For example, both the ERE "cd" and the ERE "(cd)" are matched by the third and fourth character of the string "abcdefabcdef".

## Context

This is a duplicate extraction of the concatenation rule from SHALL-09-04-06-002. Adjacent ERE atoms form a concatenation that matches the sequential concatenation of the strings each atom matches. Parentheses around a concatenation do not change what is matched—they only create a subexpression group. `cd` and `(cd)` match identically. The ERE engine must implement concatenation as implicit juxtaposition of atoms.
