## Extracted Text

> Outside of "$(...)" and "${...}" the <backslash> shall retain its special meaning as an escape character (see 2.2.1 Escape Character (Backslash)) only when immediately followed by one of the following characters: $ ` \ <newline> or by a double-quote character that would otherwise be considered special (see 2.6.4 Arithmetic Expansion and 2.7.4 Here-Document).

## Context

**Behavior:** Duplicate extraction of the backslash-in-double-quotes rule (same spec lines 148-155 as SHALL-19-02-03-007). Outside of `$(...)` and `${...}`, backslash inside double-quotes retains escape meaning ONLY before:
- `$` (prevents parameter/command/arithmetic expansion)
- `` ` `` (prevents command substitution)
- `\` (literal backslash)
- `<newline>` (line continuation—both characters removed)
- `"` that would otherwise be special (per 2.6.4 Arithmetic Expansion and 2.7.4 Here-Document)

Before any other character, `\` is literal and both characters are preserved.

**Applies to:** All shell modes. Does NOT apply inside nested `$(...)` (where unquoted rules govern) or inside `${...}` (which has its own rules per SHALL-19-02-03-003/008).

**Spec reference:** Section 2.2.3, lines 148-155 in `utilities/V3_chap02.html`. Cross-refs: 2.2.1 Escape Character, 2.6.4, 2.7.4.
