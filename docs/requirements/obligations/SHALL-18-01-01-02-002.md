## Extracted Text

> The following functionality of the fork() function defined in the System Interfaces volume of POSIX.1-2024 shall be available on all systems conforming to this volume of POSIX.1-2024:: Independent processes shall be capable of executing independently without either process terminating.

## Context

Independent processes created via `fork()` shall be capable of executing independently without either process terminating. After `fork()`, both the parent (shell) and child process must be able to continue running concurrently. Neither should be forced to terminate just because the other exists. For `meiksh`, this means background jobs (`cmd &`), pipeline components, and subshells must all execute independently of the parent shell process. The parent shell continues to accept and execute commands while children run. See POSIX Utilities Chapter 1, Section 1.1.1.2 (utilities/V3_chap01.html line 74).
