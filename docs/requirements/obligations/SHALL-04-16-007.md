## Extracted Text

> If a symbolic link is encountered during pathname resolution, the behavior shall depend on whether the pathname component is at the end of the pathname and on the function being performed. If all of the following are true, then pathname resolution is complete:: The pathname has no trailing <slash>.

## Context

This is the second of three conditions under which a symbolic link is NOT followed: the pathname must have no trailing slash. If a pathname ends with a trailing slash (e.g., `link/`), the symlink is always followed and must resolve to a directory. This interacts with the shell's `cd` builtin: `cd link/` always follows the symlink even in `-P` mode's initial resolution, and requires the target to be a directory.

See: `basedefs/V1_chap04.html` §4.16 Pathname Resolution.
