## Extracted Text

> A BRE or ERE that shall match a concatenation of single characters or collating elements.

## Context

**Behavior:** A "BRE (ERE) matching multiple characters" is defined as a BRE or ERE that shall match a concatenation of single characters or collating elements.

**Shell relevance:** In shell pattern matching, the concatenation of pattern elements matches the concatenation of the characters they each match. For example, `a?b` matches a three-character string where the first is `a`, the second is any character, and the third is `b`. The `*` wildcard matches zero or more characters (a concatenation of zero or more single-character matches).
