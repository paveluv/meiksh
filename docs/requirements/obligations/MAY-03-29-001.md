## Extracted Text

> A signal that is not attributable to a specific thread. Examples are signals sent via kill(), signals sent from the keyboard, and signals delivered to process groups. Being asynchronous is a property of how the signal was generated and not a property of the signal number. All signals may be generated asynchronously.

## Context

**Definition — moderate shell relevance.** Defines "asynchronously-generated signal" as a signal not attributable to a specific thread. Examples: signals sent via `kill()`, keyboard-generated signals (Ctrl+C → SIGINT, Ctrl+Z → SIGTSTP), and signals delivered to process groups. Any signal number may be generated asynchronously.

**Shell impact:** The shell must handle asynchronously-generated signals correctly. Key scenarios: SIGINT/SIGQUIT from the keyboard, SIGCHLD from child process termination, SIGTSTP/SIGTTIN/SIGTTOU for job control. The shell's signal handlers must be async-signal-safe. The `trap` builtin allows users to set handlers for these signals. The `kill` builtin generates them.
