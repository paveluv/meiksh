## Extracted Text

> Except as otherwise stated (by the descriptions of any invoked utilities or in interactive mode), standard error shall be used only for diagnostic messages.

## Context

The `sh` utility itself (not its individual builtins) must use standard error **only** for diagnostic messages, **except** as otherwise stated by:

1. **Descriptions of invoked utilities**: builtins and external commands may write to stderr as their own spec allows.
2. **Interactive mode**: in interactive mode, the shell writes prompts (PS1, PS2) to stderr, mail notifications to stderr, and job status notifications to stderr. These are not considered violations of this rule.

This means the shell's own non-interactive stderr output (syntax errors, command-not-found messages, redirection errors, etc.) must be diagnostic in nature. The shell must not write informational or progress messages to stderr outside of interactive mode contexts.
