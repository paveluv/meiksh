## Extracted Text

> where the fields shall be as follows:: One of the following strings (in the POSIX locale): Running Indicates that the job has not been suspended by a signal and has not exited. Done Indicates that the job completed and returned exit status zero. Done(code) Indicates that the job completed normally and that it exited with the specified non-zero exit status, code, expressed as a decimal number. Stopped Indicates that the job was suspended by the SIGTSTP signal. Stopped (SIGTSTP) Indicates that the job was suspended by the SIGTSTP signal. Stopped (SIGSTOP) Indicates that the job was suspended by the SIGSTOP signal. Stopped (SIGTTIN) Indicates that the job was suspended by the SIGTTIN signal. Stopped (SIGTTOU) Indicates that the job was suspended by the SIGTTOU signal. The implementation may substitute the string Suspended in place of Stopped. If the job was terminated by a signal, the format of <state> is unspecified, but it shall be visibly distinct from all of the other <state> formats shown here and shall indicate the name or description of the signal causing the termination.

## Context

**Behavior:** The `<state>` field must be one of these strings in the POSIX locale:

| State string | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `Running` | Job is active (not suspended, not exited) |
| `Done` | Job completed with exit status 0 |
| `Done(code)` | Job completed with non-zero exit status `code` (decimal) |
| `Stopped` | Suspended by SIGTSTP |
| `Stopped (SIGTSTP)` | Suspended by SIGTSTP (explicit signal form) |
| `Stopped (SIGSTOP)` | Suspended by SIGSTOP |
| `Stopped (SIGTTIN)` | Suspended by SIGTTIN |
| `Stopped (SIGTTOU)` | Suspended by SIGTTOU |

**Implementation options:**
- "Suspended" may be used instead of "Stopped".
- For signal-terminated jobs, the format is unspecified but must be visibly distinct from all the above formats and must indicate the signal name or description.
