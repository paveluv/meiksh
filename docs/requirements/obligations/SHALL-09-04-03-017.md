## Extracted Text

> The <circumflex> shall be special when used as an anchor (see 9.4.9 ERE Expression Anchoring ). The <circumflex> shall signify a non-matching list expression when it occurs first in a list, immediately following a <left-square-bracket> (see 9.3.5 RE Bracket Expression).

## Context

This is a duplicate extraction of the rule from SHALL-09-04-03-011. The `^` character has two special contexts: (1) anchor for start-of-string/subexpression matching (Section 9.4.9), and (2) negation in bracket expressions when `^` appears immediately after `[` (i.e., `[^...]`). In all other positions, `^` is an ordinary character matching itself. The ERE engine must correctly distinguish these contexts.
