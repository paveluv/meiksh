## Context

**Job creation from lists:** When job control is enabled, the shell creates jobs when it executes a list (see Section 2.9.3 "Lists" in `utilities/V3_chap02.html#tag_19_09_03`). A "list" is a sequence of AND-OR lists separated by `;` or `&`. The shell creates one or more jobs from a list in the following forms (detailed in SHALL-19-11-008 and SHALL-19-11-009):
1. A single asynchronous AND-OR list (terminated by `&`) → one background job
2. One or more sequentially executed AND-OR lists followed by at most one asynchronous AND-OR list → foreground job for the sequential part, then optionally a background job

The implementation must maintain a job table that tracks each job's state, process group ID, job number, and constituent processes.
