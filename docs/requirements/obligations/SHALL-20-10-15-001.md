## Extracted Text

> If job control is disabled, the bg utility shall exit with an error and no job shall be placed in the background.

## Context

If job control is disabled (i.e., `set -m` is not in effect, or the shell is non-interactive without explicit `set -m`), the `bg` builtin **shall** exit with an error status and no job **shall** be placed in the background. The shell must check whether job control is enabled before attempting to resume any job. If disabled, `bg` must write a diagnostic to stderr and return >0 without modifying any job state.
