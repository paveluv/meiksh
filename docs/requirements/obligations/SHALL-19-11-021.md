## Extracted Text

> Each background job (whether suspended or not) shall have associated with it a job number and a process ID that is known in the current shell execution environment. When a background job is brought into the foreground by means of the fg utility, the associated job number shall be removed from the shell's background jobs list and the associated process ID shall be removed from the list of process IDs known in the current shell execution environment.

## Context

**Job number and process ID tracking:** Each background job (whether running or suspended) shall have:
1. A **job number** — a small positive integer assigned sequentially or reused, displayed as `[N]` in job status messages.
2. A **process ID** — the PID of the process group leader, known in the current shell execution environment (available via `$!` for the most recent background job).

When a background job is brought to the foreground by `fg` (see `utilities/fg.html`):
- The job number shall be removed from the shell's background job list.
- The associated process ID shall be removed from the list of known PIDs in the current shell execution environment.

The implementation must maintain a job table data structure mapping job numbers to process group IDs, job states (running, stopped, done), and the list of member PIDs.
