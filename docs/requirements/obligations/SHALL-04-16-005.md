## Context

Symbolic link handling during pathname resolution is critical for the shell, particularly for:

- **`cd` builtin**: The `-L` (logical) vs. `-P` (physical) options control whether symlinks are followed or resolved. With `-L`, the shell tracks the logical path through symlinks; with `-P`, it resolves symlinks to physical paths.
- **`.` (dot) builtin**: When sourcing a file, symlinks in the path are followed by default.
- **`test -L`/`test -h`**: These operators check if a pathname is a symbolic link without following it.
- **Pathname expansion**: Symlinks to directories are followed during globbing.

This obligation establishes that symlink resolution depends on (1) whether the symlink is the last component and (2) whether the operation acts on the symlink itself. The three conditions for pathname resolution to be "complete" (not following the symlink) are listed in the next three obligations (SHALL-04-16-006 through SHALL-04-16-008).

See: `basedefs/V1_chap04.html` §4.16 Pathname Resolution.
