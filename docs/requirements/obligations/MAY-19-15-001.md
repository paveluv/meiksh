## Context

**Implementation may make any utility a built-in.**

The implementation **may** choose to make any utility a built-in (executing it within the shell process rather than fork+exec). For example, `echo`, `test`, `printf`, `cd`, `pwd` are commonly implemented as built-ins even though they are not special built-ins.

However, special built-in utilities differ from regular built-ins in two ways (detailed in SHALL-19-15-002 through SHALL-19-15-005):
1. Errors in special built-ins may cause the shell to abort; errors in regular built-ins shall not.
2. Variable assignments preceding special built-ins persist in the current environment; those preceding regular built-ins do not.

The "may make any utility built-in" freedom means the shell can optimize common commands by not forking, but must preserve the distinct error/assignment semantics for special vs. regular built-ins.
