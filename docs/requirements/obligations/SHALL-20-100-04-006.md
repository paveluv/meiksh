## Extracted Text

> The following options shall be supported:: Do not treat a <backslash> character in any special way. Consider each <backslash> to be part of the input line.

## Context

**Behavior required:** When `-r` is specified, backslash (`\`) has no special meaning:
- `\<newline>` does NOT cause line continuation; the backslash is included in the input and the newline terminates the line.
- `\<char>` does NOT escape the following character; both the backslash and the character are literal input.
- Backslash characters are NOT removed during processing.
- Backslashes do NOT prevent IFS splitting.

**Condition:** When the `-r` option is given to `read`.

**Implementation detail:** With `-r`:
1. Read until the delimiter (newline or `-d delim`) or EOF — no continuation.
2. Skip all backslash-related processing during input reading.
3. During field splitting, all characters (including backslashes) are treated as unquoted, meaning IFS characters always act as delimiters even if preceded by backslash.
