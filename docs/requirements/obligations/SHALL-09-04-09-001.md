## Context

The ERE engine must treat `^` and `$` as anchors in all positions outside bracket expressions. Inside bracket expressions, they are literal characters. As anchors: `^` constrains the match to start at the beginning of the string (or subexpression), and `$` constrains the match to end at the end of the string (or subexpression). In EREs (unlike BREs), `^` and `$` are anchors wherever they appear outside bracket expressions—not just at the start/end of the entire expression. For example, `a^b` is valid but can never match because `a` prevents `^b` from starting at position 0. See SHALL-09-04-09-002 through 005 for specific `^` and `$` anchoring rules.
