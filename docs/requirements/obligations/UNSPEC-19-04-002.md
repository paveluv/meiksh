## Extracted Text

> When used in circumstances where reserved words are recognized (described above), all words whose final character is a <colon> (':') are reserved; their use in those circumstances produces unspecified results.

## Context

**Behavior:** In reserved-word-recognition positions, ALL words whose final character is a colon (`:`) are reserved and produce unspecified results when used in those positions. This is a blanket reservation that applies to any word ending in `:`.

**Applies to:** All shell modes during parsing.

**Examples of affected words:** `foo:`, `bar:`, `label:`, `default:`, etc. — any word ending in `:` when used in a position where reserved words are recognized (first word of a command, after a reserved word other than `case`/`for`/`in`, etc.).

**Exception:** The `:` (colon) utility itself (a single colon with no preceding characters) is a special built-in, not covered by this rule since it doesn't have a colon as its "final character" — it IS the colon. (Actually `:` is a single character that ends in `:`, so it would be covered, but it has separately defined behavior as a special built-in.)

**Implementation choice:** For a strict POSIX shell, the simplest approach is to treat words ending in `:` as ordinary WORD tokens (since the spec says results are unspecified, any behavior is conforming). The implementation could also emit a warning or error.

**Spec reference:** Section 2.4, lines 433-434 in `utilities/V3_chap02.html`.
