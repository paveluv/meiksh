## Extracted Text

> [Case statement termination] When the TOKEN is exactly the reserved word esac, the token identifier for esac shall result. Otherwise, the token WORD shall be returned.

## Context

**Grammar Rule 4 (Case statement termination):** In the grammar production for `case_clause`, when the parser expects the closing `esac` keyword, if the TOKEN is exactly the reserved word `esac`, it shall produce the `Esac` token identifier. Otherwise, the TOKEN shall produce WORD (which would be a case pattern or other word within the case body).

This rule ensures that `esac` is recognized as a reserved word only at positions in the grammar where the case statement could end. In other positions within the case body (e.g., as a pattern or argument), `esac` is treated as a regular word unless it appears at a case-terminating position.
