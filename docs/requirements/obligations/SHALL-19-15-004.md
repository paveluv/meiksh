## Extracted Text

> An error in a special built-in utility may cause a shell executing that utility to abort, while an error in a regular built-in utility shall not cause a shell executing that utility to abort. (See 2.8.1 Consequences of Shell Errors for the consequences of errors on interactive and non-interactive shells.) If a special built-in utility encountering an error does not abort the shell, its exit value shall be non-zero.

## Context

**Duplicate of SHALL-19-15-002: special built-in errors may abort shell; regular built-in errors shall not.**

Same obligation as SHALL-19-15-002. Errors in special built-ins may cause shell abort (depending on interactive/non-interactive per 2.8.1); errors in regular built-ins shall not abort the shell. If a special built-in error doesn't abort, exit value shall be non-zero. See SHALL-19-15-002 for full details.
