## Extracted Text

> Subsequently, the shell shall change the foreground process group associated with its controlling terminal when a foreground job is running as noted in the description below.

## Context

**Foreground process group management during execution:** After initialization, whenever the shell runs a foreground job, it shall change the terminal's foreground process group ID to the process group of the job being executed (via `tcsetpgrp()`). This allows the foreground job's processes to read from and write to the terminal without receiving SIGTTIN/SIGTTOU signals.

The shell must restore the foreground process group to its own process group when the foreground job completes or is suspended (see SHALL-19-11-020). This obligation establishes the ongoing responsibility, while the detailed mechanics are in subsequent obligations.
