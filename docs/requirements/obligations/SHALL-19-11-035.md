## Extracted Text

> When a background job completes or is terminated by a signal, an interactive shell shall write a message to standard error, formatted as described by the jobs utility (without the -l option) for a job that completed or was terminated by a signal, respectively, at the following time:: If set -b is enabled, the message shall be written immediately after the job completes or is terminated.

## Context

**Background job done/terminated notification — `set -b` enabled (parent obligation):** When `set -b` is enabled and a background job completes or is terminated by a signal, the message shall be written **immediately after** the event occurs. "Immediately" means as soon as the shell can process the SIGCHLD or the next `waitpid` call detects the change, without waiting for the prompt loop.

The implementation should use a SIGCHLD handler or check `waitpid(WNOHANG)` frequently when `-b` is set.
