## Extracted Text

> If any part of word is quoted, not counting double-quotes outside a command substitution if the here-document is inside one, the delimiter shall be formed by performing quote removal on word, and the here-document lines shall not be expanded. Otherwise:: The delimiter shall be the word itself.

## Context

When no part of the here-document delimiter `word` is quoted, the delimiter is the word itself (no quote removal needed since there are no quotes to remove). See 2.7.4 Here-Document in utilities/V3_chap02.html.

**Behavior required:**
- If `word` is entirely unquoted (e.g., `<<EOF`), then the delimiter used to locate the terminating line is exactly `word` as written.
- This is the simple case: `<<EOF` means the delimiter is the literal string `EOF`.

**Contrast with quoted case:** When `word` contains any quoting (e.g., `<<'EOF'`, `<<\EOF`), the delimiter is formed by quote removal (see SHALL-19-07-04-002).
