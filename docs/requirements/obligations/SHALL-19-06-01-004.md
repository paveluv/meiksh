## Extracted Text

> The pathname that replaces the tilde-prefix shall be treated as if quoted to prevent it being altered by field splitting and pathname expansion; if a <slash> follows the tilde-prefix and the pathname ends with a <slash>, the trailing <slash> from the pathname should be omitted from the replacement. If the word being expanded consists of only the <tilde> character and HOME is set to the null string, this produces an empty field (as opposed to zero fields) as the expanded word.

## Context

**Behavior:** The replacement pathname from tilde expansion must be immune to further field splitting and pathname (glob) expansion — as if every character in the replacement were quoted. Additionally:
1. If the original word has a `/` immediately after the tilde-prefix AND the resolved pathname already ends with `/`, the trailing `/` from the pathname should be omitted (to avoid double-slash like `//bin`).
2. If the word is just `~` and `HOME=""` (set to empty string), the result is one empty field, not zero fields. This is significant because field splitting normally removes empty unquoted fields.

**Conditions:** Applies after the tilde-prefix has been resolved to a pathname (either from `$HOME` or from `getpwnam()`).

**Inputs/Outputs:**
- Input: resolved pathname string from tilde expansion
- Output: the pathname is marked as "quoted" internally so it is not subject to IFS field splitting or glob expansion in later expansion stages

**Error handling:** No error conditions specific to this obligation.

**Spec reference:** See 2.6.1 Tilde Expansion in utilities/V3_chap02.html, lines 765–769. Note the trailing-slash rule is currently "should" (not "shall"), but a future version may make it mandatory.
