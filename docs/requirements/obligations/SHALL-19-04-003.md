## Extracted Text

> This recognition shall only occur when none of the characters is quoted and when the word is used as:: The first word of a command

## Context

**Behavior:** Reserved word recognition position 1: The word is the **first word of a command**. This means the first word after a command separator (`\n`, `;`, `&&`, `||`, `|`, `&`), after an opening `(` or `{`, or at the very start of input. This is the most common position where reserved words are recognized.

**Applies to:** All shell modes during parsing.

**Examples:**
- `if true; then echo yes; fi` — `if`, `then`, `fi` are all first words of their respective commands.
- `echo if` — `if` is the second word (an argument to `echo`), so it is NOT recognized as a reserved word.
- `{ echo hello; }` — `{` is recognized at command start, `}` is recognized at command start (after `;`).

**Implementation detail:** The parser tracks "expecting command name" state. When in this state, tokens matching reserved words are classified as reserved word tokens. Otherwise, they are WORD tokens.
