## Extracted Text

> If a process that the shell is waiting for is part of a foreground job that was started as a foreground job and is stopped by a catchable signal (SIGTSTP, SIGTTIN, or SIGTTOU):: If the currently executing AND-OR list within the list comprising the foreground job consists of a single pipeline in which all of the commands are simple commands, the shell shall either create a suspended job consisting of at least that AND-OR list and the remaining (if any) AND-OR lists in the same list, or create a suspended job consisting of just that AND-OR list and discard the remaining (if any) AND-OR lists in the same list.

## Context

**Foreground job suspension — simple pipeline case (parent obligation):** When a process the shell is waiting for is part of a foreground job (originally started as foreground) and is stopped by a catchable signal (SIGTSTP from Ctrl+Z, SIGTTIN, or SIGTTOU):

If the currently executing AND-OR list consists of a **single pipeline where all commands are simple commands**, the shell shall either:
- (a) Create a suspended job consisting of at least that AND-OR list AND the remaining AND-OR lists in the same list, OR
- (b) Create a suspended job consisting of just that AND-OR list, discarding remaining AND-OR lists.

The implementation has a choice between (a) preserving the rest of the list for later `fg` continuation, or (b) discarding it. Option (a) is more user-friendly. See SHALL-19-11-024 for the detail version.
