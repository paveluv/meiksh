## Extracted Text

> The following rules shall be used to construct EREs matching multiple characters from EREs matching a single character:

## Context

This obligation establishes that the ERE engine must support constructing multi-character matching patterns from single-character atoms using the rules enumerated in Section 9.4.6. These rules cover: (1) concatenation, (2) the `+` repetition operator (one or more), (3) the `*` repetition operator (zero or more), (4) the `?` operator (zero or one), (5) interval expressions `{m}`, `{m,}`, `{m,n}`, and (6) the `?` repetition modifier for non-greedy matching. All of these must be implemented as specified. The behavior of multiple adjacent duplication symbols produces undefined results.
