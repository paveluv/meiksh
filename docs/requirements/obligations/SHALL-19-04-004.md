## Extracted Text

> This recognition shall only occur when none of the characters is quoted and when the word is used as:: The first word following one of the reserved words other than case, for, or in

## Context

**Behavior:** Reserved word recognition position 2: The word is the first word following one of the reserved words OTHER THAN `case`, `for`, or `in`. After these three specific reserved words, the next word is NOT checked for reserved word recognition (because it serves a different grammatical role: `case WORD`, `for NAME`, `in WORD...`).

**Applies to:** All shell modes during parsing.

**Examples:**
- `if if_cmd; then ...` — the second `if_cmd` is in the first-word position after `if`, so if it were `if`, it would be recognized as a reserved word (nested if).
- `case $x in ...` — the word after `case` (`$x`) is NOT in reserved-word position; `in` after `$x` is checked per SHALL-19-04-005.
- `for i in ...` — `i` after `for` is NOT in reserved-word position; `in`/`do` after `i` is checked per SHALL-19-04-006.

**Implementation detail:** After emitting a reserved word token (except `case`, `for`, `in`), set the parser state to "expecting command/reserved word" for the next token.

**Spec reference:** Section 2.4, line 399 in `utilities/V3_chap02.html`.
