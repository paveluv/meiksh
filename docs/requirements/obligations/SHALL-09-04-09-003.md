## Extracted Text

> An ERE can be limited to matching expressions that begin or end a string; this is called "anchoring". The <circumflex> and <dollar-sign> special characters shall be considered ERE anchors when used anywhere except inside a bracket expression. This shall have the following effects:: When not inside a bracket expression, a <dollar-sign> ('$') shall anchor the expression or subexpression it ends to the end of a string; such an expression or subexpression can match only a sequence ending at the last character of a string. For example, the EREs "ef$" and "(ef$)" match "ef" in the string "abcdef", but fail to match in the string "cdefab", and the ERE "e$f" is valid, but can never match because the 'f' prevents the expression "e$" from matching ending at the last character.

## Context

Outside bracket expressions, `$` anchors the expression or subexpression it ends to the end of the string. A `$`-anchored expression can only match a sequence ending at the last character of the input string. Key behaviors: (1) `ef$` matches "ef" only at the end of the string. (2) `(ef$)` also matches "ef" at the end (anchoring works inside subexpressions). (3) `e$f` is syntactically valid but can never match, because `f` follows the `$` anchor, preventing `e$` from matching at the end. The ERE engine must enforce end-of-string anchoring for `$` wherever it appears outside bracket expressions.
