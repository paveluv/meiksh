## Extracted Text

> An ERE special character has special properties in certain contexts. Outside those contexts, or when preceded by an unescaped <backslash>, such a character shall be an ERE that matches the special character itself. The extended regular expression special characters and the contexts in which they shall have their special meaning are as follows:: The <right-parenthesis> shall be special when matched with a preceding <left-parenthesis>, both not inside a bracket expression.

## Context

The `)` character has special meaning (closing a group/subexpression) only when it corresponds to a preceding `(` and neither the `(` nor the `)` is inside a bracket expression. If no matching `(` exists, or either parenthesis is inside a bracket expression, then `)` is not special. The ERE engine must maintain a parenthesis stack/counter to correctly pair `(` and `)` tokens, excluding those inside bracket expressions from the pairing.
