## Extracted Text

> where the fields shall be as follows:: The character '+' identifies the job that would be used as a default for the fg or bg utilities; this job can also be specified using the job_id %+ or "%%". The character '-' identifies the job that would become the default if the current default job were to exit; this job can also be specified using the job_id %-. For other jobs, this field is a <space>. At most one job can be identified with '+' and at most one job can be identified with '-'. If there is any suspended job, then the current job shall be a suspended job. If there are at least two suspended jobs, then the previous job also shall be a suspended job.

## Context

**Behavior:** The `<current>` field rules:

1. **`+` (current job):** The default job for `fg` and `bg`. Also addressable as `%+` or `%%`. At most one job has this marker.
2. **`-` (previous job):** Would become current if the `+` job exits. Also addressable as `%-`. At most one job has this marker.
3. **Space:** All other jobs.

**Suspended job priority rules:**
- If any suspended job exists, the current job (`+`) SHALL be a suspended job.
- If at least two suspended jobs exist, the previous job (`-`) SHALL also be a suspended job.

**Implementation guidance:** Track the most recently suspended job as current (`+`) and the second most recently suspended as previous (`-`). When a `+` job exits, `-` becomes `+`.

**Spec reference:** POSIX.1-2024, `jobs` STDOUT, lines 124-128 of `utilities/jobs.html`.
