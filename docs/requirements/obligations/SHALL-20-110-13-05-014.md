## Extracted Text

> Perform pathname expansion (see 2.6.6 Pathname Expansion) on the current bigword, up to the largest set of characters that can be matched uniquely. If the bigword contains none of the characters '?', '*', or '[', an <asterisk> ('*') shall be implicitly assumed at the end. This maximal expansion then shall replace the original bigword in the command line, and the cursor shall be placed after this expansion. If the resulting bigword completely and uniquely matches a directory, a '/' character shall be inserted directly after the bigword. If some other file is completely matched, a single <space> shall be inserted after the bigword. After this operation, sh shall be placed in insert mode.

## Context

**Behavior:** In vi command mode, `\` shall perform pathname expansion (see 2.6.6 Pathname Expansion in `utilities/V3_chap02.html`) on the current bigword, completing up to the largest uniquely matchable prefix (tab-completion style).

**Details:**
- If the bigword contains none of `?`, `*`, or `[`, an `*` is implicitly appended.
- The maximal unique expansion replaces the original bigword in the command line.
- The cursor is placed after the expansion.
- If the expansion completely and uniquely matches a directory, a `/` is inserted after it.
- If it completely matches some other file, a single space is inserted after it.
- After this operation, the shell enters insert mode.

**Implementation:** Extract the bigword under/before cursor, perform glob expansion, find the longest common prefix of all matches, replace the bigword, position cursor, and switch to insert mode.
