## Extracted Text

> A terminal may have a foreground process group associated with it. This foreground process group plays a special role in handling signal-generating input characters, as discussed in 11.1.9 Special Characters.

## Context

**Behavior (optional):** A terminal may have a foreground process group. This group receives signals generated by special input characters (INTR→SIGINT, QUIT→SIGQUIT, SUSP→SIGTSTP). The foreground process group is the group that the terminal considers "active" for signal delivery.

**Conditions:** The foreground process group is relevant when job control is enabled. The shell sets and manages the foreground process group using tcsetpgrp().

**Shell relevance:** Critical for job control. The shell must set the foreground process group to the process group of the currently executing foreground job, and restore it to the shell's own process group when the job completes or is suspended.
