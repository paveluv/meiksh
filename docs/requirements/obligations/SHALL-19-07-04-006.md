## Extracted Text

> If any part of word is quoted, not counting double-quotes outside a command substitution if the here-document is inside one, the delimiter shall be formed by performing quote removal on word, and the here-document lines shall not be expanded. Otherwise:: Any <backslash> characters in the input shall behave as the <backslash> inside double-quotes (see 2.2.3 Double-Quotes). However, the double-quote character ('"') shall not be treated specially within a here-document, except when the double-quote appears within "$()", "``", or "${}".

## Context

Defines backslash and double-quote handling within unquoted here-document bodies. See 2.7.4 Here-Document in utilities/V3_chap02.html and 2.2.3 Double-Quotes (tag_19_02_03).

**Behavior required — backslash:**
- Backslash characters in the here-document body behave as they would inside double-quotes.
- Per 2.2.3, inside double-quotes, backslash retains special meaning only before: `$`, `` ` ``, `"`, `\`, and `<newline>`. Before any other character, the backslash is literal.
- In a here-document, this means: `\$` produces literal `$`, `\\` produces literal `\`, `\`` `` produces literal `` ` ``, `\<newline>` is line continuation. But `\n`, `\t`, etc. produce literal `\n`, `\t` (backslash is preserved).

**Behavior required — double-quote:**
- The double-quote character (`"`) SHALL NOT be treated specially within a here-document body. It is literal text.
- Exception: double-quotes ARE treated specially when they appear inside `$()`, `` ` ` ``, or `${}` constructs (which are being expanded as part of parameter/command substitution).

**Key implication:** A here-document body is like being inside double-quotes for backslash purposes, but `"` itself is not a quoting character in the body.
