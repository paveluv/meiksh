## Extracted Text

> A sequence of zero or more characters shall be said to be matched by a BRE or ERE when the characters in the sequence correspond to a sequence of characters defined by the pattern.

## Context

**Behavior:** A sequence of zero or more characters shall be said to be "matched" by a BRE or ERE when the characters in the sequence correspond to a sequence of characters defined by the pattern.

**Shell relevance:** This definition of "matched" applies to the shell's pattern matching as well (section 2.14 references section 9.3.5 for bracket expressions). When the shell evaluates `case` patterns, filename globbing, or `[[ ... ]]` pattern matches, matching means the input character sequence corresponds to the pattern's defined character sequence.
