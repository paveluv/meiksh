## Context

**Behavior:** In the `-p` format, each floating-point number shall be expressed in seconds with the following precision constraints:
1. The precision may be less than the default 6 digits of `%f`, but must be precise enough to accommodate the clock tick granularity (e.g., if 60 ticks/sec, at least 2 digits after the radix character).
2. At least one digit shall follow the radix character, even if it is always zero.
3. The implementation may append whitespace and additional information after the format.
4. The implementation may prepend a single empty line before the format.

**Implementation:** Use at least 2 decimal places (to handle typical clock tick granularities). A common choice is 2-3 decimal places. Always print at least one decimal digit.
