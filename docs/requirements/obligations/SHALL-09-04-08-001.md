## Context

The ERE engine must implement operator precedence from highest to lowest as follows: (1) Collation-related bracket symbols (`[==]`, `[::]`, `[..]`) — highest. (2) Escaped characters (`\<special>`). (3) Bracket expressions (`[]`). (4) Grouping (`()`). (5) Single-character-ERE duplication (`*`, `+`, `?`, `{m,n}`). (6) Concatenation (implicit adjacency). (7) Anchoring (`^`, `$`). (8) Alternation (`|`) — lowest. This means, for example, `abba|cde` matches "abba" or "cde" (not "abbade" or "abbcde"), because concatenation binds more tightly than alternation. The parser must respect this precedence when building the ERE syntax tree.
