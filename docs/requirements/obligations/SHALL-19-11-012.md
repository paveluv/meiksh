## Extracted Text

> For a list consisting of one or more sequentially executed AND-OR lists followed by at most one asynchronous AND-OR list, the whole list shall form a single foreground job up until the sequentially executed AND-OR lists have all completed execution, at which point the asynchronous AND-OR list (if any) shall form a background job as described above.

## Context

**Mixed sync/async list job creation:** For a list like `cmd1; cmd2; cmd3 &`:
1. The entire list forms a single **foreground** job while the sequential AND-OR lists (`cmd1; cmd2`) execute.
2. Once all sequential AND-OR lists complete, the trailing asynchronous AND-OR list (`cmd3 &`), if present, shall form a **background** job as described in SHALL-19-11-011.

The foreground job transitions: first the shell waits for `cmd1`, then `cmd2` (sequentially), then kicks off `cmd3` as a background job. The foreground job is complete when all sequential parts finish. The implementation must handle this transition cleanly, including proper process group management.
