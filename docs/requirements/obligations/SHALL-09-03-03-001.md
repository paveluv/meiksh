## Extracted Text

> A BRE special character has special properties in certain contexts. Outside those contexts, or when preceded by an unescaped <backslash>, such a character is a BRE that matches the special character itself. The BRE special characters and the contexts in which they have their special meaning are as follows:: The <period>, <left-square-bracket>, and <backslash> shall be special except when used in a bracket expression (see 9.3.5 RE Bracket Expression). An expression containing a '[' that is unescaped and is not part of a bracket expression produces undefined results.

## Context

**Behavior:** In BRE context, the period (`.`), left-square-bracket (`[`), and backslash (`\`) shall be special characters except when used inside a bracket expression. An expression containing an unescaped `[` that is not part of a bracket expression produces undefined results.

**Shell relevance:** In shell pattern matching, the analogous special characters are `?`, `*`, `[`, and (in some contexts) `\`. Inside bracket expressions, these lose their special meaning per section 9.3.5. The shell must ensure that an unmatched `[` in a pattern that is not part of a valid bracket expression is handled — POSIX says this produces undefined results, so the shell may treat it as a literal `[` or report an error.
