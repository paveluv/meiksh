## Extracted Text

> This recognition shall only occur when none of the characters is quoted and when the word is used as:: The third word in a for command (only in and do are valid in this case)

## Context

**Behavior:** Reserved word recognition position 4: The third word in a `for` command. In `for NAME in ...` or `for NAME do ...`, the third word position can be `in` or `do`. ONLY `in` and `do` are valid reserved words in this position.

**Applies to:** All shell modes during parsing.

**Grammar context:** `for NAME [in WORD...] do ... done`. After `for` (first word) and the loop variable NAME (second word), the parser expects either `in` (to introduce the word list) or `do` (if the word list is omitted, defaulting to `"$@"`). Newlines may appear between NAME and `in`/`do`.

**Implementation detail:** After recognizing `for` and consuming the variable name token, check if the following token (after skipping newlines) is the unquoted word `in` or `do`. Classify accordingly. Any other word here may indicate a syntax error (depending on whether newlines intervene).

**Spec reference:** Section 2.4, line 401 in `utilities/V3_chap02.html`.
