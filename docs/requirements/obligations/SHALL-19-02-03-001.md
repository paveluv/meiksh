## Extracted Text

> Enclosing characters in double-quotes ("") shall preserve the literal value of all characters within the double-quotes, with the exception of the characters backquote, <dollar-sign>, and <backslash>, as follows:

## Context

**Behavior:** When the shell encounters a double-quoted string (`"..."`), it must preserve the literal value of every character inside the quotes, with three exceptions: backquote (`` ` ``), dollar-sign (`$`), and backslash (`\`). All other characters—including single-quotes, spaces, tabs, newlines, glob characters (`*`, `?`, `[`), pipe (`|`), semicolon (`;`), etc.—lose their special meaning and are treated as literal text.

**Applies to:** All shell modes (interactive and non-interactive), during the word expansion phase after token recognition.

**Key implementation detail:** The tokenizer must track the double-quote state so that characters like `*`, `?`, `[`, `|`, `;`, `&`, `(`, `)`, `<`, `>`, and whitespace are not interpreted as operators, glob patterns, or token delimiters while inside double-quotes. Field splitting (2.6.5) must not split on IFS characters within double-quoted regions, and pathname expansion (2.6.6) must not expand glob patterns within double-quoted regions.

**Exceptions to preservation (handled by sibling obligations):** `$` retains expansion-introducing meaning (see SHALL-19-02-03-002/003/008), `` ` `` retains command-substitution meaning (see SHALL-19-02-03-004/005/009), and `\` retains limited escape meaning (see SHALL-19-02-03-006/007/010).
