## Extracted Text

> [Redirection from here-document] Quote removal shall be applied to the word to determine the delimiter that is used to find the end of the here-document that begins after the next <newline>.

## Context

**Grammar Rule 3 (Here-document delimiter):** The word following `<<` or `<<-` is the here-document delimiter. Quote removal shall be applied to this word to determine the actual delimiter string used to find the end of the here-document body (which begins after the next newline).

Critically, whether any part of the delimiter word was quoted determines whether the here-document body undergoes expansion:
- If **any** character in the delimiter word was quoted (by `\`, `'`, or `"`), the here-document body is treated literally (no expansion).
- If the delimiter word was **entirely unquoted**, the body undergoes parameter expansion, command substitution, and arithmetic expansion (but not field splitting or pathname expansion).

The implementation must: (1) record the raw delimiter word at parse time, (2) apply quote removal to get the actual delimiter, (3) record whether quoting was present to control body expansion at execution time, and (4) read input lines until a line consisting solely of the delimiter is found.
