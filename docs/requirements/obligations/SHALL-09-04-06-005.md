## Extracted Text

> The following rules shall be used to construct EREs matching multiple characters from EREs matching a single character:: When an ERE matching a single character or an ERE enclosed in parentheses is followed by the special character <question-mark> ('?'), together with that <question-mark> it shall match what zero or one consecutive occurrences of the ERE would match. For example, the ERE "b?c" matches the second character in the string "acabbbcde".

## Context

The `?` operator must match zero or one occurrence of the preceding single-character ERE or parenthesized ERE. It makes the preceding atom optional. The match is greedy by default (prefers one occurrence over zero when either could lead to an overall match). Example: `b?c` in "acabbbcde" matches "c" at position 2 (zero `b`s followed by `c`). The `?` operator must not be confused with the `?` repetition modifier (non-greedy suffix) described in SHALL-09-04-06-007.
