## Extracted Text

> A mechanism by which a process or thread may be notified of, or affected by, an event occurring in the system. Examples of such events include hardware exceptions and specific actions by processes. The term signal is also used to refer to the event itself.

## Context

**Definition — direct shell relevance.** Defines "signal" as a mechanism to notify a process/thread of system events. Examples include hardware exceptions and actions by other processes. "Signal" also refers to the event itself.

**Shell impact:** Signals are central to shell operation:
- The `trap` builtin sets signal handlers for the shell process
- The `kill` builtin sends signals to processes/jobs
- Job control uses SIGTSTP, SIGCONT, SIGTTIN, SIGTTOU
- The shell handles SIGCHLD for child process reaping
- SIGINT and SIGQUIT have special handling in interactive shells
- Signal disposition (SIG_DFL, SIG_IGN) is inherited across exec and managed per the spec
- `$?` encodes signal-based termination (128+signum)
