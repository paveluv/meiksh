## Extracted Text

> An ERE special character has special properties in certain contexts. Outside those contexts, or when preceded by an unescaped <backslash>, such a character shall be an ERE that matches the special character itself. The extended regular expression special characters and the contexts in which they shall have their special meaning are as follows:: *+?{

## Context

The characters `*`, `+`, `?`, and `{` are ERE special characters that serve as duplication/repetition operators. They have special meaning everywhere except inside a bracket expression. Inside a bracket expression, they match themselves literally. When preceded by an unescaped backslash outside a bracket expression, they match themselves literally. See SHALL-09-04-03-007 for additional constraints on their placement and undefined-result conditions.
