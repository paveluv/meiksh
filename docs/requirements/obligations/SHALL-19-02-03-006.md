## Extracted Text

> Enclosing characters in double-quotes ("") shall preserve the literal value of all characters within the double-quotes, with the exception of the characters backquote, <dollar-sign>, and <backslash>, as follows:: \

## Context

**Behavior:** Inside double-quotes, the backslash character (`\`) retains its special meaning as an escape character, but only in limited circumstances (specified in the sibling obligation SHALL-19-02-03-007/010). This is the third of three exceptions to the double-quote literal-preservation rule.

**Applies to:** All shell modes, during tokenization and expansion of double-quoted strings.

**Key distinction:** Unlike outside quotes where `\` can escape any character, inside double-quotes `\` only functions as an escape before specific characters. Before any other character, the `\` is literal (preserved in the output alongside the character that follows it).

**Spec reference:** Section 2.2.3, line 147 in `utilities/V3_chap02.html`. The specific characters before which `\` retains meaning are listed in SHALL-19-02-03-007.
