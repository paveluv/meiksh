## Extracted Text

> Define characters to be classified as letters. In the POSIX locale, only characters in the classes upper and lower shall be included. In a locale definition file, no character specified for the keywords cntrl, digit, punct, or space shall be specified. Characters classified as either upper or lower are automatically included in this class.

## Context

**Behavior:** The `alpha` keyword defines the set of letter characters:
- In the POSIX locale: union of `upper` and `lower` (A-Z, a-z).
- In other locales: all `upper` and `lower` characters are automatically included, plus additional locale-specific letters (e.g., characters with diacritics that are neither uppercase nor lowercase).
- Constraint: No character in `alpha` may also be in `cntrl`, `digit`, `punct`, or `space`.

**Shell relevance:** The shell uses the `alpha` class for:
- `[[:alpha:]]` bracket expression in pattern matching.
- The `alpha` class determines what characters are considered "letters" in locale-aware contexts.
- In POSIX locale, `[[:alpha:]]` matches A-Za-z only.
