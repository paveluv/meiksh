## Extracted Text

> Some devices require control operations, other than the operations that are common to most devices (such as read(), write(), open(), and close()), but because the device belongs to a class that is not present in the majority of systems, standardization of a device-specific application program interface (API) for controlling it has not been practical. The driver for such a device may respond to the write() function to transfer data to the device or the read() function to collect information from the device. The interpretation of the information is defined by the implementor of the driver.

## Context

This obligation describes special device drivers and their interaction with standard I/O operations. For the shell, this means that redirecting I/O to or from special device files (e.g., `/dev/null`, `/dev/tty`, or custom character devices) may have device-specific behavior. The shell's redirection mechanism must work with any file, including special device files, by passing read/write operations through to the OS. No special shell code is needed — the shell uses standard `open()`/`read()`/`write()`/`close()` syscalls which work with device files transparently.

See: `basedefs/V1_chap04.html` §4.21 Special Device Drivers.
