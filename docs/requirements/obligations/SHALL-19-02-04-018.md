## Extracted Text

> These <backslash>-escape sequences shall be processed (replaced with the bytes or characters they yield) immediately prior to word expansion (see 2.6 Word Expansions) of the word in which the dollar-single-quotes sequence occurs.

## Context

**Behavior:** The backslash-escape sequences in `$'...'` must be processed (replaced with the bytes/characters they yield) **immediately prior to word expansion** (2.6 Word Expansions) of the word containing the `$'...'` sequence. This means:

1. Escape processing happens AFTER tokenization but BEFORE parameter expansion, command substitution, arithmetic expansion, field splitting, and pathname expansion.
2. The result of escape processing is treated as quoted text—it is not subject to further expansion or splitting.
3. The timing is important: the escape-processed bytes become part of the word that then undergoes the normal expansion pipeline, but since they originated from a quoted context, they are protected from field splitting and globbing.

**Applies to:** All shell modes.

**Implementation detail:** In the expansion pipeline, process `$'...'` escape sequences at the same stage as quote removal would normally happen for other quoting forms, or in a dedicated pre-expansion pass. The key constraint is that it must happen before any of the 2.6 expansions.

**Spec reference:** Section 2.2.4, lines 224-225 in `utilities/V3_chap02.html`. Cross-ref: 2.6 Word Expansions.
