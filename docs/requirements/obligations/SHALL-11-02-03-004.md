## Extracted Text

> The delay bits specify how long transmission stops to allow for mechanical or other movement when certain characters are sent to the terminal. In all cases a value of 0 shall indicate no delay. If OFILL is set, fill characters shall be transmitted for delay instead of a timed delay. This is useful for high baud rate terminals which need only a minimal delay. If OFDEL is set, the fill character shall be DEL; otherwise, NUL.

## Context

**Behavior:** The delay bits in `c_oflag` specify how long transmission pauses when certain characters are sent to the terminal (for mechanical movement). A value of 0 shall indicate no delay in all cases. If OFILL is set, fill characters shall be transmitted instead of a timed delay (useful for high baud rate terminals). If OFDEL is set, the fill character shall be DEL (0x7F); otherwise, NUL (0x00).

**Relevance to meiksh:** The `stty` builtin must support the OFILL and OFDEL flags, as well as the delay type masks (NLDLY, CRDLY, TABDLY, BSDLY, VTDLY, FFDLY). These are largely relevant for legacy hardware terminals but must be configurable per POSIX.
