## Extracted Text

> The quote character sequence <dollar-sign> single-quote and the single-character quote characters (<backslash>, single-quote, and double-quote) that were present in the original word shall be removed unless they have themselves been quoted. Note that the single-quote character that terminates a <dollar-sign> single-quote sequence is itself a single-character quote character.

## Context

Quote removal is the final step in word expansion (after tilde expansion, parameter expansion, command substitution, arithmetic expansion, field splitting, and pathname expansion). See 2.6.7 in utilities/V3_chap02.html.

**Behavior required:**
- Remove all unquoted quoting characters that were present in the original word:
  - `$'...'` (dollar-sign single-quote sequence): remove the `$'` prefix and the terminating `'`
  - `\` (backslash): remove the backslash character itself (the escaped character remains)
  - `'...'` (single-quotes): remove the surrounding single-quote characters
  - `"..."` (double-quotes): remove the surrounding double-quote characters
- If a quoting character has itself been quoted (e.g., `\\` or `"'"` or `'\''`), it is NOT removed — it is retained as literal data.
- The single-quote that terminates a `$'...'` sequence is itself a single-character quote character, so it follows the same rule.

**Important post-condition:** Even after quote removal, the shell must still remember which characters were originally quoted. This "quote memory" is needed for:
- Pattern matching in `case` statements (quoted characters match literally, not as pattern metacharacters; see 2.9.4.3 and 2.14)
- Preventing field splitting of results (quoted portions are not subject to IFS splitting)

**Ordering:** This is the last expansion step. It applies to every word in the command line after all other expansions are complete.
