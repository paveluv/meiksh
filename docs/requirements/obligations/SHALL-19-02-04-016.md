## Extracted Text

> A sequence of characters starting with a <dollar-sign> immediately followed by a single-quote ($') shall preserve the literal value of all characters up to an unescaped terminating single-quote ('), with the exception of certain <backslash>-escape sequences, as follows:: The behavior of an unescaped <backslash> immediately followed by any other character, including <newline>, is unspecified.

## Context

**Behavior:** Inside `$'...'`, a backslash immediately followed by any character that is NOT one of the recognized escape letters (`"`, `'`, `\`, `a`, `b`, `e`, `f`, `n`, `r`, `t`, `v`, `c`, `x`, or an octal digit) — including a literal newline — produces unspecified behavior.

**Applies to:** All shell modes during `$'...'` escape processing.

**Implementation choice:** The implementation may choose any behavior for unrecognized `\X` sequences. Common approaches: (a) treat `\` as literal (emit both `\` and the character), (b) emit just the character (strip the backslash), (c) emit just the backslash (ignore the character), or (d) report an error. Approach (a) is most compatible with existing shells.
