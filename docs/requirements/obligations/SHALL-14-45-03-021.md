## Extracted Text

> The <signal.h> header shall declare the SIGRTMIN and SIGRTMAX macros, which shall expand to positive integer expressions with type int, but which need not be constant expressions. These macros specify a range of signal numbers that are reserved for application use and for which the realtime signal behavior specified in this volume of POSIX.1-2024 is supported. The signal numbers in this range do not overlap any of the signals specified in the following table.

## Context

`SIGRTMIN` and `SIGRTMAX` must be defined in `<signal.h>` as positive integer expressions of type `int` (need not be compile-time constants). They define the range of realtime signals reserved for application use. For `meiksh`, realtime signals are relevant to the `kill` and `trap` builtins—the shell must be able to send and trap realtime signals in the range `SIGRTMIN` to `SIGRTMAX`. The shell's signal name-to-number mapping must include these, and `trap` must accept them. The realtime signal range must not overlap with standard signals (SIGABRT, SIGTERM, etc.).
