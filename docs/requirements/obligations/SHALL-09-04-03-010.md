## Extracted Text

> An ERE special character has special properties in certain contexts. Outside those contexts, or when preceded by an unescaped <backslash>, such a character shall be an ERE that matches the special character itself. The extended regular expression special characters and the contexts in which they shall have their special meaning are as follows:: ^

## Context

The circumflex `^` is an ERE special character. Its special meaning is context-dependent: it acts as an anchor when used to match the beginning of a string (see Section 9.4.9), and it signifies a non-matching list expression when it is the first character after `[` in a bracket expression (see Section 9.3.5). Outside of these two contexts, when preceded by an unescaped backslash, it matches the literal `^` character. See SHALL-09-04-03-011 for the detailed anchoring and bracket-expression rules.
