## Extracted Text

> The EXIT condition shall occur when the shell terminates normally (exits), and may occur when the shell terminates abnormally as a result of delivery of a signal (other than SIGKILL) whose trap action is the default.

## Context

**Behavior:** The EXIT condition:
1. Must occur when the shell terminates **normally** (via `exit`, end of input, etc.).
2. **May** also occur when the shell terminates abnormally due to delivery of a signal (other than SIGKILL) whose trap action is the default.

**Implementation:** Always execute the EXIT trap on normal exit. Optionally execute it on signal-caused termination (recommended for robustness).

**Applies to:** All shell modes.
