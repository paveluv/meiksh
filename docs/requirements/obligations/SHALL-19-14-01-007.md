## Context

**Three special pattern characters: `?`, `*`, `[` when unquoted, unescaped, outside bracket expressions.**

The three characters `?`, `*`, and `[` **shall** have special meaning in patterns when all three conditions are met:
1. They are unquoted (not inside single-quotes, double-quotes, or $'...')
2. They are unescaped (not preceded by `\`)
3. They are not inside a bracket expression (`[...]`)

When these conditions are met, they activate pattern matching behavior rather than matching themselves literally. The specific behavior of each is defined in SHALL-19-14-01-008 through SHALL-19-14-01-016.

When any of these characters is quoted or escaped, it matches itself as an ordinary character.
