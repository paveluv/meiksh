## Extracted Text

> If the redirection operator is "<<" or "<<-", the word that follows the redirection operator shall be subjected to quote removal; it is unspecified whether any of the other expansions occur. For the other redirection operators, the word that follows the redirection operator shall be subjected to tilde expansion, parameter expansion, command substitution, arithmetic expansion, and quote removal. Pathname expansion shall not be performed on the word by a non-interactive shell; an interactive shell may perform it, but if the expansion would result in more than one word it is unspecified whether the redirection proceeds without pathname expansion being performed or the redirection fails.

## Context

This obligation defines which word expansions apply to the `word` token in different redirection operators. See 2.7 Redirection in utilities/V3_chap02.html.

**Behavior required — here-document operators (`<<`, `<<-`):**
- The `word` (delimiter) shall be subjected to quote removal only.
- It is unspecified whether tilde expansion, parameter expansion, command substitution, or arithmetic expansion are performed on the delimiter word. (The safe implementation choice is to not expand the delimiter word beyond quote removal.)

**Behavior required — all other redirection operators (`<`, `>`, `>>`, `>|`, `<&`, `>&`, `<>`):**
- The `word` shall be subjected to: tilde expansion, parameter expansion, command substitution, arithmetic expansion, and quote removal.
- Pathname expansion shall NOT be performed on `word` in a non-interactive shell.
- In an interactive shell, pathname expansion MAY be performed, but if the expansion yields more than one word, the behavior is unspecified (the redirection may proceed without pathname expansion, or it may fail).

**Implementation recommendation:** For simplicity and portability, do not perform pathname expansion on redirection words even in interactive mode. If you do, ensure single-word results work and multi-word results produce an error.
