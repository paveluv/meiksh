## Extracted Text

> Two EREs separated by the special character <vertical-line> ('|') shall match a string that is matched by either. For example, the ERE "a((bc)|d)" matches the string "abc" and the string "ad". Single characters, or expressions matching single characters, separated by the <vertical-line> and enclosed in parentheses, shall be treated as an ERE matching a single character.

## Context

The ERE engine must implement the alternation operator `|`. Two EREs separated by `|` match any string that either ERE would match individually. Example: `a((bc)|d)` matches "abc" (via `a` then `bc`) and "ad" (via `a` then `d`). Important: when single-character EREs are alternated and enclosed in parentheses, the result is considered a single-character ERE for purposes of applying duplication operators. For example, `(a|b)+` treats `(a|b)` as a single-character ERE. Alternation has the lowest precedence of all ERE operators (below concatenation and anchoring), as specified in Section 9.4.8.
