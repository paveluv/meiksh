## Extracted Text

> When double-quotes are used to quote a parameter expansion, command substitution, or arithmetic expansion, the literal value of all characters within the result of the expansion shall be preserved.

## Context

**Behavior:** When double-quotes enclose a parameter expansion (`${...}` or `$name`), command substitution (`$(...)` or `` `...` ``), or arithmetic expansion (`$((...))`), the literal value of all characters within the **result** of that expansion must be preserved. This means:

- The result of the expansion is not subject to field splitting (2.6.5) or pathname expansion (2.6.6).
- Characters like `*`, `?`, `[`, spaces, tabs in the expansion result are treated as literal text, not as glob patterns or field separators.
- For example: if `$var` contains `* foo`, then `"$var"` expands to the single word `* foo` (no glob, no splitting).

**Exception:** The special parameter `"$@"` expands to separate words (one per positional parameter) even inside double-quotes—but each individual word still has its literal value preserved.

**Applies to:** All shell modes during word expansion.

**Implementation detail:** After performing the expansion, the resulting characters must be marked as "quoted" (or equivalent) so that subsequent field splitting and pathname expansion skip them.

**Spec reference:** Section 2.2.3, line 158 in `utilities/V3_chap02.html`.
