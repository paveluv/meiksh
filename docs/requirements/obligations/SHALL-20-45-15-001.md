## Extracted Text

> If job control is disabled, the fg utility shall exit with an error and no job shall be placed in the foreground.

## Context

When job control is disabled (i.e., `set +m` is in effect, or the shell is non-interactive and hasn't explicitly enabled job control), the `fg` builtin must: (1) Exit with an error status (>0). (2) NOT attempt to move any job to the foreground. (3) Write a diagnostic message to stderr indicating that job control is not enabled. This is a hard requirement — `fg` must check the job control state before attempting any job manipulation. Reference: POSIX.1-2024 §fg, CONSEQUENCES OF ERRORS (tag_20_45_15).
